What is Neuroplasticity?

Neuroplasticity is the term used in psychology to say that our brain is infact malleable and adapts to challenges or the environment.

How does the understanding of neuroplasticity benefit people?

Well it benefits people in understanding that it is possible to improve with repeated practice and or recall of information. It also provides them with the knowledge of how to improve, because they will realise that all skills and knowledge come from repeated practice or recall and not something they were born with.

How will I engage in the principles of neuroplasticity for my own benefit?

I will engage in the principles of neuroplasticity by using strategies such as spaced repetition and spaced recall to ensure that my brain retains the knowledge and it is fully embedded as chunks of knowledge that I can use.

How can we improve neuroplasticity?

Well we can improve neuroplasticity by incorporation a more growth mindset, and learning the power of the word yet, for example instead of saying “I’m not good at math” you can say “I’m not good at math yet.”. This implies that as we put forth effort we will become better at that activity. We can also prove this to ourselves, by practicing skills that we aren’t particularly good at, and as you get better you improve your confidence for learning.

What is a Growth Mindset?

To put it simply growth mindset is the perspective or belief that we can improve our intelligence by putting forth effort. In saying that we now then see failure not as a negative state but more of a learning opportunity and we see challenges not as an indication of inaptitude but more of a signal that we are stretching our abilities and allowing ourselves to grow.

Did anything surprise your or change for you?

Not really, I have been interested in these topics since I was child. I have always been curious on how I can improve my learning and improve my skills and this was a particular topic that I had come across.

How will I integrate a growth mindset to my learning journey?

I will integrate a growth mindset to my learning journey by approaching challenges rather than running away from them, and I will also repeatedly practice and recall to improve my abilities.

How will my understanding of a growth mindset and neuroplasticity affect my learning plan and strategies?

Well it will improve it in the sense that It will make me more confident in approaching challenges. It will also allow me to reframe failure as a neutral state or a stepping stone to improving.
